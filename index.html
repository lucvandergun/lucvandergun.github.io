About Me

Hi! I'm Luc, and I'm passionate about technology. I mean this in two ways: I love making and designing technology as much as I enjoy reflecting on its role in our lives. More specifically, I'm passionate about both programming and philosophy. At first glance, these domains might seem to have little in common. But not only are both innovative design and critical reflection crucial for creating responsible technology, they also share in being forms of writing. As is visible in both my programming projects and the philosophy articles, I'm especially drawn to systematic writing: designing, implementing and analysing structures, architectures and frameworks. Whether I'm building software or exploring its meaning, I enjoy arranging and fine-tuning multiple dimensions into coherent, useful and hopefully even elegant systems.

Biography

I grew up in a small village in the Veluwe, the Netherlands. After highschool, I began a Bachelor's degree in Artificial Intelligence at Radboud University in Nijmegen. To widen my horizon and to take on an additional challenge, I also pursued a Bachelor's in Philosophy. Fascinated by the intersection of these fields, I published an essay on this topic and went on to enroll in the Research Master's in Philosophy, focusing specifically on the philosophy of technology. Alongside my studies, I worked as a teaching assistant for a variety of courses in the AI programme, ranging from programming principles and techniques to philosophy-related courses that consider the societal and scientific impact of AI. Outside of academia, I've actively pursued my passion for technology through numerous coding projects, with a strong emphasis on game development. Most of my development work has been in C#, Python and Java, though I also have experience with HTML/CSS, JavaScript, Scala, SQL and several other languages.

You can explore some of the highlights of my work in the portfolio section. For a more detailed overview of my academic and professional experience, you can download my CV here (Dutch).

Selected Work

Coding Projects

Retro Racers

Since late 2023, I've been developing Retro Racers, a tribute to the classic game Lego Racers originally created by High Voltage Software. This collaborative project is built in the Unity framework, where my (C#) programming responsibilities are paired with my teammate's work in 3D modelling.

Specifically, my role involves designing and implementing the core gameplay systems, as well as making sure that the many components at hand integrate smoothly. Some of the key features I've developed so far include a fine-tuned car controller, power-up mechanics, and a system for building custom cars. Technically, this project has brought many learning points, ranging from balancing realistic physics with arcade-style responsiveness to working with a complex, multifaceted programming framework.

Caverns of Azzandra

(github.io | itch.io)

From 2020 to 2023, I developed Caverns of Azzandra, a solo "roguelike" project inspired by classic dungeon crawlers. It is built entirely from scratch using the basic MonoGame framework in C#, from an implementing an anchoring gameplay loop to designing a versatile rendering system.

Its principal technical feats include elaborate class inheritance architectures with robust saving and loading mechanisms, advanced lighting and pathfinding algorithms, and procedural level generation. To support variety and customisation in each of these systems, while keeping them editable and controllable, I implemented an extensive architecture for JSON-based data handling. Caverns of Azzandra was released in Beta on itch.io and received multiple updates before development concluded.
